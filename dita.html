DARWIN INFORMATION TYPING ARCHITECTURE (DITA) & EXTENSIBLE MARKUP LANGUAGE (XML)


Week 11 of WRIT5662: Writing with Digital Technologies, we dove into DITA, where we downloaded a DITA-specific text editor and worked on structured authoring with DITA.

DITA

DITA is an open XML standard that is developed and maintained by the OASIS DITA Technical Committee. Some of the benefits of DITA XML is its ability for content to be written in small, tagged chunks, making it easy to facilitate reuse of content across different formats and documents. DITA also differentiates between types of information and allows the use of conditional text. The three main information types include: Tasks, Concepts, and References.

XML

Extensible Markup Language (XML) is quite similar to Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) in that there are standard rules defining its format, however XML is different in that there are no predefined tags. What is unique about XML is that it requires you to define your own tags.
